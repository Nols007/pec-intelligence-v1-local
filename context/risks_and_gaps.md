# PEC Intelligence: Risks and Gaps

## Technical Risks
- **Integration Complexity:** Integrating multiple utility management platforms into a single system may lead to unforeseen technical challenges.
- **Scalability Concerns:** The prototype may not be designed to handle large-scale data or user loads, which could impact future scalability.
- **AI Insight Accuracy:** Simulated AI insights may not accurately reflect real-world scenarios, leading to potential misinterpretations.

## Design and Usability Gaps
- **Mobile Responsiveness:** Ensuring the application works seamlessly across all mobile devices, especially iPhone Safari, is crucial.
- **User Experience:** The complexity of role-based views and dynamic scope options may confuse users if not intuitively designed.

## Operational Risks
- **Alert System Reliability:** Simulated alerts may not accurately represent real operational signals, affecting the perceived reliability of the system.
- **Data Privacy and Security:** Handling sensitive utility data requires robust security measures, which may not be fully implemented in a prototype.

## Strategic Risks
- **Alignment with Strategic Vision:** The prototype must clearly demonstrate the strategic value and future potential of PEC Intelligence to executive leadership.
- **Stakeholder Buy-in:** Gaining support from all user roles and stakeholders is essential for successful adoption.

## Development Risks
- **Timeline and Resource Constraints:** Limited time and resources may impact the quality and completeness of the prototype.
- **Dependency Management:** Introducing unnecessary dependencies could complicate future development and maintenance.
